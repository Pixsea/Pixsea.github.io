About Me


I graduated from the University of California Irvine in June of 2022, with a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Game Science. I specialize in game design and programming, and I've worked with projects across a variety of genres. In my free time, I enjoy creating small passion projects in Unity and Unreal Engine 5 to learn more about each engine. I also have been learning how to mod Android apps to make custom mods for Plants vs Zombies 2. Beyond that, I enjoy drawing (though I'm not very good at it), as well as playing Wii Fit and doing weekly zoom yoga with my family to stay in shape.

My favorite games include almost anything from Nintendo, especially Mario and Kirby games. I also enjoy games that give a lot of personality to their characters, such as Undertale, Plants Vs. Zombies, Cuphead, Punch Out, Overwatch, or Team Fortress 2. As far as multiplayer games go, I enjoy Super Smash Bros, and team shooters like Splatoon, Rainbow Six Siege, Valorant or Marvel Rivals.

  Work Experience


Foundry 6

Currently, I am working at Foundry 6 as a Unity Developer and Designer. I work 100 Day Siege, a medieval VR game where you defend your castle against large monster hordes. I led the creation of the game's second world, a set of levels revolving around defending against armies of arcane mushroom creatures. I also work on an AR app focused on helping people during DIY projects. I work with the app's AI to identify and highlight different parts of the user’s workspace, including dangerous objects, and tools they need. I have also implemented AR tools that allow users to measure objects, and mark up their environment.

Foundry 6 Website






Genies

I was a Game Developer and Designer at Genies. While there, I created games for their mobile gaming platform. I pitched and created games that utilized the platform's user avatars. I integrated the player’s personality traits and custom avatars, and created mechanics that revolve around both, to give each person a unique gameplay experience that fits their personality. I was heavily involved in assessing the company’s new game creation platform, testing its limits, and documenting bugs whenever it was updated.

Genies Website






Unscrolled

I worked at Unscrolled as a Mobile Game Designer and Developer. My primary duties involved playtesting the game to identify areas where players could get stuck, and designing ways to guide them through it. I created level design documents to map out these plans, while also ensuring that the levels were both intuitive to navigate, and engaging to the players. I later built out these levels in Unity, fine tuning specific segments to create a smooth experience. I was also heavily involved with the work on the game’s camera and movement systems, better adapting them for mobile devices to make the user experience more fluid.





The Coder School

I am currently working at The Coder School in Irvine, California. Dedicated to teaching kids how to code, The Coder School employs a non-static curriculum designed to teach kids regardless of their knowledge or skill set. Working as a remote coach, it's my job to develop curriculum tailored to their speed, abilities, and interests. I utilize different techniques, such as metaphors and analogies, to gain understanding among students.
