About Me


I graduated from the University of California Irvine in June of 2022, with a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Game Science. I specialize in game design and programming, and I've worked with projects across a variety of genres. In my free time, I enjoy creating small passion projects in Unity, and learning how to work with Unreal Engine 5. Beyond that, I enjoy drawing (though I'm not very good at it), and doing Wii Fit yoga to stay in shape.

I’m currently working part time at The Coder School, where we teach kids how to code using a personalized curriculum. We work in 1-on-1 and 2-on-1 sessions to help kids learn at their own pace. I was also the lead designer and a programmer on Patient Zero, a VR game created with Unity, in association with the Biology department at the University of California Irvine.

My favorite games include almost anything from Nintendo, especially Mario and Kirby games. I also enjoy games that give a lot of personality to their characters, such as Undertale, Plants Vs. Zombies, Cuphead, or Team Fortress 2. As far as multiplayer games go, I enjoy Super Smash Bros, and team shooters like Rainbow Six Siege or Valorant.

The Coder School

I'm currently working part time at The Coder School in Irvine, California. Dedicated to teaching kids how to code, The Coder School employs a non-static curriculum designed to teach kids regardless of their knowledge or skill set. Working as a remote coach, it's my job to develop curriculum tailored to their speed, abilities, and interests. I utilize different techniques, such as metaphors and analogies, to gain understanding among students.
